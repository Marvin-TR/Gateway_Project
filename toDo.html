Attractions in Mexico!

Touristic Spots!

National Antropology Museum

The national museum of antropologya is a touristic place for people of all ages that are interested in arts. It cost $80.00 mxn pesos ($3.64 usd) to enter and it displays multiple anthropology art pieces.

Aquarium Michin in Guadalajara

Aquarium Michin is a huge aquarium that has a great variety of sea creatures. It's target age is for people of all ages and the price is fixed on $220 mxn pesos ($10.02 usd) for everyone.

Day of the Dead

The Day of the Dead starts on October 31st Day of the Dead is one of Mexico's many traditions. This tradition is aimed for all the family to enjoy together as it only happens once a year on October 31st. The festival takes place in the streets of Mexico City and it is free for everyone.

Basilica of Guadalupe

The Basilica of Guadalupe is a Roman Catholic church, basilica, and National shrine of Mexico which houses the cloak containing the image of Our Lady of Guadalupe. Being a church it is for all ages and the guided tours are providedfree of charge.

Water Park Inbursa

Water Park Inbursa is a fun water park aimed for people of all ages since it has a great variety of pools. The kids enter for free while adults have to pay according to their height, 3ft-4ft pay $180 mxn pesos ($8.20 usd) and 4ft+ pay $225 pesos ($10.25 usd).

Teotihuacán

Teotihuacán is an ancient Mesoamerican city located in a sub-valley of the Valley of Mexico most remarkably, the pyramids. This city has a huge history behind it and anyone is welcome to come learn about them personally. The price to enter is $75 mxn pesos ($3.42 usd) and free on Sundays for locals.

Cancún Underwater Museum

This museum is a very unique once since it is completely underwater. It has a restriction for kids under the age of 4 and for pregnant women but everyone else is welcome. The price to dive into this museum varies but just a basic tour will cost $1,500 mxn (68.33 usd) to everyone except children, which pay half.

Island of the Dolls

The Island of the Dolls is an island that is decorated with multiple dolls hanging from trees. The area has thousands of people, but this small island is home to hundreds of terrifying dolls. Their severed limbs, decapitated heads, and blank eyes adorn trees. While it is pretty creepy, people of all ages are welcomed and the price for a tour is around $200 mxn ($15.5 usd).
